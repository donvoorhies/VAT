The Vertical Tracking Angle (VTA) Alignment Tool (The "VAT"-app)

What is the Vertical Tracking Angle (VTA)?:

In the realm of turntables and vinyl playback, achieving optimal sound quality is a pursuit that often delves into the minutiae of setup and calibration.
One crucial factor in this pursuit is the Vertical Tracking Angle (VTA).
VTA refers to the angle at which the stylus of a phonograph cartridge meets the surface of a vinyl record during playback.
This angle profoundly impacts the fidelity of audio reproduction, influencing aspects such as tonal balance, imaging, and tracking ability.

Using the Vertical Tracking Angle (VTA) Alignment Tool app:

Ensuring the correct alignment of the tonearm's angle is paramount in attaining ideal VTA.
The alignment tool app is specifically designed for VTA adjustments can be indispensable in this process.
Here's how to effectively utilize this app:

  1. Placement: Begin by placing and holding your phone - with the VAT-app running - onto the plinth of the turntable, ensuring it sits securely and level - and the headshell and/or tonearm is seen on the display.
  2. Tonearm Positioning: Lower the tonearm onto a vinyl record already placed on the platter, allowing the stylus to make gentle contact with it's surface.
  3. Observation: While keeping the phone with the running VAT-app stationary, observe the angle at which the tonearm runs parallel with the app's gridlines on the display.
  4. Adjustment: Depending on the design of your tonearm, use the appropriate adjustment mechanism to fine-tune the VTA angle, so that tonearm runs parallel with the VAT-app's displayed gridlines.
    This adjustment may involve raising or lowering the rear of the tonearm to achieve having the headshell/tonearm running parallel with the app's gridlines, thus obtainingthe desired VTA.
  5. Reevaluation: After making adjustments, place and hold the tonearm onto the VAT-app again on the plinth to confirm the new VTA angle.
    Repeat adjustments as necessary until the desired VTA is achieved.
